Problem 1: Modular Arithmetic (15 points)

(a) Compute 7^{142} mod 11.

(b) Find all x in {0, 1, ..., 12} satisfying 5x ≡ 3 (mod 13).

(c) Prove that for any prime p and any integer a not divisible by p,
    a^{p-1} ≡ 1 (mod p).
    You may use any theorem stated in lecture.

Problem 2: Proof by Induction (20 points)

Prove by strong induction that every integer n ≥ 2 can be written as a product of primes.

More precisely, prove the statement P(n): "n can be expressed as p_1 · p_2 · ... · p_k for some primes p_1, ..., p_k (k ≥ 1)."

(a) State and prove the base case.

(b) State the inductive hypothesis clearly.

(c) Complete the inductive step. Be sure to handle the case where n+1 is prime and the case where n+1 is composite separately.

Problem 3: Graphs (20 points)

Consider a connected undirected graph G = (V, E) with |V| = n vertices.

(a) Prove that if G is a tree, then |E| = n - 1.

(b) Suppose G has exactly n edges. Prove that G contains at least one cycle.
    Hint: What happens if you remove edges one at a time?

(c) An Eulerian path is a path that uses every edge exactly once.
    Prove that a connected graph has an Eulerian path if and only if
    it has exactly 0 or 2 vertices of odd degree.
    You may prove only one direction for full credit.

Problem 4: Discrete Probability (15 points)

A bag contains 5 red balls, 3 blue balls, and 2 green balls. You draw 3 balls without replacement.

(a) What is the probability that all three balls are the same color?

(b) What is the probability that you get exactly one ball of each color?

(c) Let X be the number of red balls drawn. Compute E[X] and Var(X).

Problem 5: RSA Encryption (15 points)

Alice sets up RSA with primes p = 5 and q = 11.

(a) Compute N and φ(N).

(b) Alice chooses encryption exponent e = 3. Verify that gcd(e, φ(N)) = 1 and compute the decryption exponent d.

(c) Bob sends the message m = 9. Compute the ciphertext c = m^e mod N.

(d) Show that Alice can recover m by computing c^d mod N.

Problem 6: Counting and Combinatorics (15 points)

(a) How many ways can you arrange the letters in the word MISSISSIPPI?

(b) A committee of 5 is chosen from 8 men and 6 women. How many committees have at least 2 women?

(c) How many bit strings of length 10 have exactly 4 ones, with no two consecutive ones?
    Hint: Think about placing ones in gaps.
