Problem 1: Modular Inverses and CRT

(a) Find the inverse of 7 modulo 15 using the extended Euclidean algorithm.
    Show each step of the algorithm.

(b) Solve the following system of congruences using the Chinese Remainder Theorem:
      x ≡ 2 (mod 3)
      x ≡ 3 (mod 5)
      x ≡ 1 (mod 7)

(c) How many solutions exist in {0, 1, ..., 104}? List them all.

Problem 2: Graph Coloring and Planarity

(a) What is the chromatic number of the complete bipartite graph K_{3,3}?
    Justify your answer.

(b) Is K_{3,3} planar? Prove your answer using Euler's formula or Kuratowski's theorem.

(c) A graph G has 8 vertices and 13 edges. Can G be planar? Justify using the
    inequality e ≤ 3v - 6.

Problem 3: Conditional Probability and Bayes' Rule

A test for a disease has the following properties:
  - Sensitivity (true positive rate): 95%
  - Specificity (true negative rate): 90%
  - Prevalence of the disease: 1%

(a) You test positive. What is the probability you actually have the disease?
    Use Bayes' theorem and show your work.

(b) You test positive on the first test and then take a second independent test,
    which also comes back positive. Now what is the probability you have the disease?

(c) Explain intuitively why the answer to (a) is so much lower than the sensitivity.

Problem 4: Counting with Restrictions

(a) How many ways can 8 distinct books be arranged on a shelf if two specific books
    (say A and B) must be next to each other?

(b) How many surjective functions are there from a set of 5 elements to a set of 3 elements?
    Use inclusion-exclusion.

(c) In how many ways can you distribute 10 identical cookies among 4 children
    if each child must get at least 1 cookie?
    Use the stars and bars method.
